The Lost Art of Letter Writing and Why It Still Matters Today
In a world dominated by instant messaging and email, handwritten letters have become rare. Yet, the process of writing and receiving a letter carries a unique emotional weight. The time, thought, and personal touch involved make letters feel more intimate and meaningful than digital messages.

Letters offer a tangible connection across time and space. They can be kept, reread, and treasured for years. Reviving the habit of letter writing can slow communication down in a good way, helping us express emotions more thoughtfully and authentically.
